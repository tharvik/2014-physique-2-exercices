\[
	\mu = \frac{Q}{\pi R^2} 2 \pi r = \frac{2 Q r}{R^2}
\]
At the center, \(r = 0 \to \mu = 0\), so the \(\vec{B}\) is 0.

--

\[
	\phi = \frac{Q}{\pi R^2}
\]
\[
	dq = \phi 2 \pi r dr
\]
\[
	dI = \frac{dq}{T}
\]
\[
	T = \frac{2 \pi}{w}
\]
\[
	d \vec{B} = \frac{\mu_0 d I}{2 r} \hat{z}
\]
\[
	\vec{B} = \int_{disk} d \vec{B} = \frac{\mu_0 u Q}{2 \pi R}
\]
